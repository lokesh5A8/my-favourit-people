My Favourite people

mahesh Babu

Mahesh Babu, born Ghattamaneni Mahesh Babu on August 9, 1975, is a leading Indian actor and producer predominantly working in Telugu cinema. Known as the "Prince of Tollywood," he is one of the highest-paid actors in India and enjoys a massive fan following. Mahesh made his acting debut as a child artist in 1979 and debuted as a lead actor with Rajakumarudu (1999). Over the years, Mahesh Babu has delivered numerous blockbuster hits such as Okkadu (2003), Pokiri (2006), Dookudu (2011), Srimanthudu (2015), and Maharshi (2019), earning critical acclaim and several awards, including multiple Filmfare Awards South. His films are known for their strong storytelling, action sequences, and emotional depth. Mahesh is celebrated for his charismatic screen presence, versatile acting, and philanthropic activities. Besides acting, he is a successful brand ambassador and producer. With a career spanning over two decades, Mahesh Babu remains one of the most influential and respected actors in Indian cinema.

Uppalapati Venkata Suryanarayana Prabhas Raju, known simply as Prabhas, is a prominent Indian actor primarily working in Telugu cinema. Born on October 23, 1979, he is one of the highest-paid actors in India and has been featured in Forbes India's Celebrity 100 list since 2015. Prabhas debuted with the film Eeswar (2002) and gained fame with Varsham (2004). He earned critical acclaim and a Nandi Award for Mirchi (2013). Prabhas achieved pan-Indian stardom with the blockbuster Baahubali series (2015, 2017), directed by S.S. Rajamouli. The sequel became the highest-grossing Indian film at the time, making him the first pan-Indian star with international recognition. He is the only Indian actor with six films opening to over ₹100 crore worldwide and the first to have a film gross over ₹1000 crore globally. Hailing from a film family, Prabhas holds a B.Tech degree and is known for his dedication, versatility, and strong fan base across India.

Jr. NTR

Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao Jr., widely known as Jr NTR, is a leading Indian actor, playback singer, and television presenter in Telugu cinema. Born on May 20, 1983, he is the grandson of legendary actor and former Andhra Pradesh Chief Minister N. T. Rama Rao. Jr NTR began his career as a child artist in the National Award-winning film Ramayanam (1996) and debuted as a lead with Student No: 1 (2001), which became a major success. He is celebrated for his versatility, powerful screen presence, and dynamic dance skills. Jr NTR has delivered several blockbusters such as Aadi (2002), Simhadri (2003), Yamadonga (2007), Baadshah (2013), Nannaku Prematho (2016), and the globally acclaimed RRR (2022). He has won multiple prestigious awards, including three Filmfare Awards South and two Nandi Awards. Known as the "Man of Masses," Jr NTR is among the highest-paid actors in India and has featured in Forbes India's Celebrity 100 list since 2012. His performances and box office appeal have made him one of the most influential stars in Indian cinema.

Kajal Agarwal

Kajal Aggarwal is a prominent Indian actress and model best known for her work in Telugu and Tamil cinema, as well as a few Hindi films. Born on June 19, 1985, in Mumbai, she began her acting career with the Hindi film Kyun! Ho Gaya Na... (2004) before making her Telugu debut with Lakshmi Kalyanam (2007). Kajal gained widespread recognition with the blockbuster Chandamama (2007) and achieved pan-Indian fame with Magadheera (2009), one of Telugu cinema’s biggest hits. Over the years, Kajal has starred in several successful films, including Darling (2010), Brindavanam (2010), Singham (2011), Thuppakki (2012), and Mersal (2017). She is celebrated for her versatile performances, charm, and screen presence. Kajal has received multiple awards and nominations, cementing her status as one of South India’s leading actresses. Her career spans over 15 years, and she remains a popular and influential figure in Indian cinema.

Anushka

Anushka Shetty, born Sweety Shetty on November 7, 1981, is a highly acclaimed Indian actress known for her work in Telugu and Tamil cinema. With a career spanning over two decades and more than 50 films, she is celebrated as one of the highest-paid and most influential South Indian actresses, often called the "Lady Superstar" of Tollywood. She made her debut with the Telugu film Super (2005) and gained early recognition with S.S. Rajamouli’s Vikramarkudu (2006). Anushka’s breakthrough came with the fantasy thriller Arundhati (2009), where her dual roles won her the Filmfare Award for Best Actress – Telugu and the Nandi Award. She continued to impress with acclaimed performances in films like Vedam, Rudhramadevi, and the blockbuster Baahubali series, where her portrayal of Devasena earned widespread praise. Anushka has received numerous awards, including three Filmfare Awards South, two Nandi Awards, and the Kalaimamani honor from Tamil Nadu. Known for her versatility and commanding screen presence, she remains a leading figure in South Indian cinema.
